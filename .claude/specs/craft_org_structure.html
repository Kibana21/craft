User journeys
Home screen
No workspace needed. Two project types + one shared library.
The insurance hierarchy — FSC → Agency Leader → District Leader → Brand Team — is real but you don't need to mirror it exactly in software. That's where complexity explodes. Instead, use two simple project types that naturally serve every level: My Projects (personal, I own it) and Team Projects (someone creates it, invites others). A Brand Library sits above both — approved content anyone can browse and remix. That's it. Three things. No workspace concept needed.
👤

Read-only for FSCs · publish-only for brand admins
Why no Workspace?
A workspace makes sense when multiple organisations use the same tool (like Slack workspaces for different companies). You have one organisation — AIA Singapore. A workspace layer just adds a login step and a management overhead with no benefit.
The "personal space" concept is already handled by My Projects. Every user opens CRAFT and sees their own home — their projects, what's shared with them, the brand library. That IS their workspace, just without the word.
Keep it to: My Projects · Shared with me · Brand Library. Those three tabs on the home screen cover every scenario you described.
Four roles — mapped to your real hierarchy
Roles control what you can publish and who you can invite — not what you can create. Everyone can create. Only leaders can share downward. Only admins can publish to the Brand Library.

The key principle — who sees what
My Projects
Only you see these. Unless you explicitly share — then only the people you shared with.
Team Projects
All members see all artifacts inside the project. The creator controls who's a member.
Brand Library
Everyone in AIA CRAFT can view and remix. Only Brand Admins can add to it.
🏢
Brand team launching a new product
Brand Admin · AIA Marketing
1
Creates a Team Project — "PAA Launch Q2 2025". Fills master brief: product, audience, period, key message, brand kit.
2
Invites 2 colleagues (L&D manager, campaign designer) to the project. They can all create artifacts inside it.
3
Team creates: training video, customer explainer, Instagram poster, agent deck — all inside one project, all inheriting the same brief.
4
Publishes the customer-facing poster and WhatsApp card to the Brand Library — now all FSCs can find and remix it.
🗺
District Leader running a team campaign
District Leader · 15 FSCs in team
1
Creates a Team Project — "D12 Young Parents Campaign June". Invites 15 FSCs in their district.
2
Remixes the PAA poster from the Brand Library — adapts it with district-specific messaging, creates a WhatsApp broadcast card.
3
FSCs inside the project can see the district assets AND create their own artifacts — personalised with their own photo and tone.
4
District Leader can see all 15 FSCs' artifacts, track what's been created, and give feedback via the chat editor.
👤
FSC creating their own personal campaign
Financial Service Consultant · individual agent
1
Opens Brand Library, finds the PAA poster their brand team published. Clicks "Remix into my project".
2
A new My Project is created automatically, with the PAA brief already loaded. They just add their own photo and pick a tone.
3
CRAFT generates a personalised version — their headshot composited, their name, compliant, brand-locked. Exported in 30 seconds.
4
They also have their own My Projects from previous months — personal campaigns they built independently without any team project.
This is what every user sees when they open CRAFT — same home screen for brand admin, district leader, and FSC. The content inside each tab is different based on their role.
